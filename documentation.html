Sexual Harassment Data Documentation

"Human trafficking involves the use of force, fraud, or coercion to obtain some type of labor or commercial sex act". (U.S. DHS, 2020). Human trafficking is an ever increasing problem world-wide and even within the United States. We believe that there needs to be more awareness for human trafficking which is why we created this data visualization. Our group analyzed and visualized two types of human trafficking: commercial sex acts and involuntary servitude.

Types of Trafficking

Commercial Sex Acts

Commercial sex acts are defined as "any sex act on account of which anything of value is given to or received by any person" (22 USC Ch. 78: Trafficking Victims Protection). These types of crimes include the following:

  • Prostitution
  • Pornography
  • Sex tourism
  • Mail order brides
  • Stripping/performing at sexual venues

Involuntary Servitude

Involuntary servitude "includes a condition of servitude induced by means of (a) any scheme, plan, or pattern intended to cause a person to believe that, if the person did not enter into or continue in such condition, that person or another person would suffer serious harm or physical restraint; or (b) the abuse or threatened abuse of the legal process" (U.S. DOS, 2020).

  • Peonage
  • Prostitution
  • Rape

Utilized Data-sets

These were the data-sets that we used in order to construct the data visualizations:

  • This first dataset looks at Commercial Sex Acts & Involuntary Servitude offenses per state. In order to design the visualizations, data from the respective attributes were extracted.
  • Commercial Sex Acts & Involuntary Servitude by State
  • This second dataset looks at both of the types of trafficking by sex/gender and age per state. In order to achieve the design for the last visualization (Sex by State), totals were taken for both the male and female attributes for juvenile and non-juvenile victims.
  • Age and Sex/Gender by State

Questions to Answer

Below are the questions we wanted to answer with our data visualization:

  • Which states are responsible for types of human trafficking?
  • What is the severity of trafficking per state by type?
  • Which gender is most susceptible to each type of trafficking?

Involuntary Servitude

















For both of these chart depictions we decided to use different shades of red. Red usually is understood as a representation for "bad things." We felt, considering the data material, this would be the most appropriate color choice.

Sex by State

For this last chart we decided to use blue and pink to convey the male and female attributes. We felt this was the most universally understood color scheme to differentiate between the two genders. Furthermore, we made the choice orient the charts side-by-side for easy cross-referencing.
